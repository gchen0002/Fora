# Novelty Research

## Short Answer

The broad idea is not completely novel. There are already platforms that help students discover internships, scholarships, mentorship, volunteer work, courses, jobs, and other career opportunities.

However, the specific angle in this project can still be novel enough for a hackathon if it is framed and demoed around access-aware matching for underrepresented and nontraditional tech learners.

## Similar Existing Products

### Tallo

Tallo helps students and early-career users find jobs, internships, scholarships, courses, career pathways, and personalized career recommendations.

Overlap:

- student profiles
- personalized recommendations
- jobs, internships, scholarships, and career paths
- career exploration

Difference:

- Tallo is broader career navigation.
- This project can focus more tightly on access barriers, inclusion needs, and opportunity fit for underrepresented tech learners.

### Goodwall

Goodwall is a youth-focused social/career platform that connects young people to education and work opportunities such as jobs, internships, courses, scholarships, volunteering, and challenges.

Overlap:

- mobile-first youth platform
- opportunity discovery
- social/community features
- education and work opportunities

Difference:

- Goodwall is closer to a social network and skills/challenges platform.
- This project can differentiate through private access profiles, transparent matching, and a feed designed specifically for inclusive tech access.

### StartBright

StartBright focuses on scholarships, grants, internships, and student opportunities with matching based on background and education level.

Overlap:

- scholarships
- grants
- internships
- student opportunity discovery
- tailored opportunity recommendations

Difference:

- StartBright appears to be a broader student opportunity directory.
- This project can be more specific about tech access, structural barriers, distance, cost, remote availability, and accommodations.

### Opportunist

Opportunist is a personalized opportunity discovery platform for students. It curates internships, volunteer programs, and networking events based on student interests and goals.

Overlap:

- personalized opportunity feed
- student-focused discovery
- internships, volunteering, and networking events
- reduces the need to search many disconnected sources

Difference:

- Opportunist is one of the closest comparisons.
- This project needs to clearly emphasize access-aware matching, underrepresented tech learners, private inclusion profiles, and transparent recommendation explanations.

### Put Me On

Put Me On connects underrepresented students and early-career professionals to internships, fellowships, entry-level jobs, mentorship, financial support, and community.

Overlap:

- underrepresented students
- STEM opportunity access
- mentorship
- financial and career barriers
- community support

Difference:

- Put Me On is strongly mission-aligned and barrier-aware.
- This project can differentiate as a product experience: short-form discovery feed, saved playlists, friend sharing, and structured matching explanations.

### Underrepresented in Tech

Underrepresented in Tech is a free database built to help underrepresented people in tech find opportunities and help others find diverse contributors, speakers, workers, and collaborators.

Overlap:

- underrepresented people in tech
- database/search model
- diversity and inclusion focus

Difference:

- It is more of a searchable database and visibility platform.
- This project can focus on proactive recommendations and user-side opportunity discovery.

## Novelty Assessment

If the product is pitched only as:

> An app that recommends scholarships, internships, hackathons, and mentorship opportunities.

Then it is not very novel. Several platforms already do this.

If the product is pitched as:

> A TikTok-style opportunity feed that matches underrepresented and nontraditional tech learners using private access needs, identity-aware eligibility, location range, cost sensitivity, accessibility support, and transparent "why this matched you" explanations.

Then it becomes much more distinctive.

## Strongest Differentiator

The strongest differentiator is access-aware matching.

Most opportunity platforms match on things like:

- interests
- major
- school year
- career goals
- resume/profile data
- location

This project should match on whether a person can realistically access and benefit from the opportunity:

- free or low-cost
- remote or nearby
- beginner-friendly
- mentorship included
- transportation support
- childcare support
- disability accommodations
- language accessibility
- code of conduct or safer-space signals
- eligibility for women, LGBTQ+, disabled, first-gen, low-income, rural, caregiver, parent, or nontraditional learners

## Recommended Pitch Framing

Existing platforms help students find opportunities. This product helps underrepresented and nontraditional learners find opportunities they are actually able to access.

It does that by matching on barriers like cost, distance, remote availability, beginner friendliness, accommodations, mentorship, and eligibility, then explaining every recommendation transparently.

## Hackathon Takeaway

The idea is novel enough for a hackathon if the demo focuses on:

- private inclusion/access profile
- access tags on opportunities
- transparent "why this matched you" explanations
- short-form opportunity feed
- save/dismiss/share actions
- playlists for organizing next steps

The product should not compete as a generic opportunity board. It should compete as an inclusive access layer for tech opportunity discovery.
